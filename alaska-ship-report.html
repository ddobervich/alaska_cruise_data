Discovery Princess ⚖️ Compare to other ships ↑

Built 2022  ·  145,000 GT
19 decks (15 guest-accessible)
Capacity: ~3,660 guests
Search sailings ↗
General Information
Decks / guest capacity 19 decks total, 15 guest-accessible; ~3,660 guests at double occupancy. 1,830 staterooms. 1,346 crew.
Pool heated? Alaska usable? Partially. 4 pools: 2 main top-deck pools, Wake View infinity pool, and adults-only Retreat Pool. Heating status of main outdoor pools not officially confirmed. 10 hot tubs — these are definitely warm and popular in Alaska. Forum consensus: most people stick to hot tubs outdoors in Alaska, not the main pools. The Watercolor Fantasy fountain show (85-jet light show) runs at night on the main pool deck.
What Kids Care About
Can kids roam ship without adult supervision? No strict "no roaming" rule, but Princess expects parents to supervise children. Teens 13–17 have their own area (Beach House) and more freedom. All under-18s must be in their staterooms by 1:00 AM. In practice, many families let older kids (10+) roam the ship in small groups; this is broadly accepted but left to parents' discretion.
Kids club self-checkout? No for ages 3–7 (Tree House): parent/guardian must sign in/out; staff have pagers to contact you.
Yes for ages 8–12 (The Lodge): kids can sign themselves in/out.
Ages 13–17 (Beach House): come and go freely, no check-in required.
Kids club activities Camp Discovery (partnership with Discovery Channel). Activities include: MythBusters-style science experiments, Alaska destination-immersive programs (local wildlife, culture), Shark Hunters interactive scavenger hunts, Animal Planet puppet and mask making. Alaska-specific: glacier science activities, Alaskan wildlife education. Hours: Sea days 9am–noon, 2–5pm, 7–10pm; Port days 8am–5pm, 7–10pm.
Self-serve ice cream hours Complimentary soft-serve ice cream station on the pool deck with daily-changing flavors, sprinkles and chocolate sauce available. Available approximately 11am–10pm (daytime/evening pool hours). Exact hours vary — check daily Patter on board.
Self-serve pizza/sliders/pasta hours Pool-deck pizza counter (hand-tossed Neapolitan-style): open approximately late morning to late night (roughly noon–midnight on sea days). World Fresh Marketplace/Horizon Court buffet (includes pizza, pasta, burgers, tacos): open 5am–11pm (midnight on some days) — serves all-day including these items.
Self-serve breakfast from buffet (without adults)? Yes. The Horizon Court / World Fresh Marketplace buffet is a self-serve format open from 5am, serving a full breakfast spread. Kids can serve themselves freely.
Pool for kids? Movies at pool? Main pool area accessible to all; Retreat Pool is adults-only. 10 hot tubs. Movies Under the Stars (MUTS): Princess's signature outdoor movie screen on the pool deck shows movies, sporting events, and shows throughout the day and evening — a great Alaska-evening activity bundled up under blankets (Princess provides them). Not ideal for Alaska swimming but fantastic for movie nights on deck.
Other onboard activities for kids Mini golf, sports courts (basketball, volleyball, paddle tennis), fitness center (age restrictions apply), Seaview Walk (top deck walking track), arcade, teen area with hot tub (ages 13–17). Alaska-specific enrichment: ranger talks, wildlife spotting, binoculars for glacier viewing.
Movies available other times? Yes. Movies Under the Stars shows movies throughout day/evening. In-room TV has an on-demand movie selection. Movies also shown in the ship's theater.
What Adults Care About
Daily / evening activities Sea days: trivia, bingo, wine tastings, cooking demos, photography seminars, dance lessons (ballroom, salsa, country line dance), art auctions, fitness classes, spa seminars, casino tournaments. Evening: production shows, comedy, live music, themed parties, casino, Movies Under the Stars, deck parties.
Entertainment (specific shows) Nightly Broadway-style productions with live orchestra and full cast. Alaska-specific programming Princess is known for: Puppies in the Piazza (Alaskan husky sled dog history), Deadliest Catch captains (Q&A with actual Bering Sea crab fishermen), North to Alaska (original show by Alaska singer-storyteller Steve Hites), Candlelight Concert: Fire & Ice (Alaska singer-songwriters, 2x per voyage), Native Alaskan storytellers speaker series. Also: magicians, illusionists, comedians, string quartets.
Timed dinner seatings? Yes (your choice). Three options: Traditional Early (~5:00pm), Traditional Late (~7:15pm) — same table/waitstaff every night. Or Anytime/Reservable Dining (5:30–10pm) — walk-in or book via the Princess MedallionClass app before sailing. Many families prefer Anytime for flexibility with kids.
Vibe with small children Princess is family-friendly but not a "kids-first" line like Disney. Alaska sailings skew slightly older than Caribbean sailings, but Discovery Princess in July still has many families. Other guests are generally tolerant and friendly. The ship is large enough that adults and families can both find their own space. Not overwhelming with small kids, but you won't find the same level of immersive kids programming as Disney or even Royal Caribbean's biggest ships.
Stateroom pluses / minuses + Nearly all outside cabins have private balconies (1,438 of 1,830 staterooms). Wake View infinity-style pool views from some cabins are stunning. Aft-facing balconies offer some of the best glacier views.
− Lifeboats obstruct views from Decks 7–8 (significant block). Deck 8 above the theater can hear noise during shows and rehearsals. Cabins below/near the aft buffet (Horizon Court) can hear chair scraping and foot traffic from 6am onward. Midship Decks 9–11 are the sweet spot for quiet + stability.
Tips & Notables
Key tips / pluses / minuses
✅ Discovery Princess is one of Princess's newest and largest ships (2022) — better facilities than Royal Princess
✅ Alaska enrichment programming (Deadliest Catch, dog sled history, Native storytellers) is a genuine highlight that other lines don't match
✅ MedallionClass technology makes dining reservations and ordering easy
✅ Movies Under the Stars on a cold Alaska night (under provided blankets) is a fan favorite
⚠️ This ship runs one-way Whittier↔Vancouver itineraries — you'll need to arrange travel to/from Whittier (train or flight) which adds logistics
❌ Main outdoor pools unlikely to be appealing in Alaska temperatures — plan on hot tubs
❌ Very large ship (3,660 guests) — can feel crowded at peak times (sailaway, port days returning)

Royal Princess does round-trip Seattle with Glacier Bay, which is one of the most scenic Princess itineraries — adults and kids alike tend to be out on deck a lot for glacier viewing. General Information

Pool for kids? Movies at pool? Lido Pools (family access, Deck 17); Wake View infinity pool. Kids excluded from The Dome. Movies Under the Stars on pool deck. Given the ship's newness, Star Princess may have additional pool-deck features not yet widely reported.
Other activities for kids As the newest Princess ship, Star Princess is expected to have the most comprehensive facilities: sports courts, mini golf, expanded arcade, teen area. Alaska enrichment programming same as fleet. Full activity listing still emerging as ship was just launched in 2025.

The sheer scale means families and adults can both find their space easily. Newest ship means freshest facilities and staff enthusiasm. Less user feedback available due to ship's recent launch.
Stateroom pluses / minuses + Newest, freshest staterooms in the Princess fleet. Deluxe Balcony cabins are ~200 sq ft + 35 sq ft balcony (notably larger than older ships). Extended balcony options. Newest technology (USB outlets everywhere, MedallionClass integration).

General Information
Decks / guest capacity 16 decks; ~4,160 guests; 2,105 staterooms; ~1,500 crew. Quantum-class ship — purpose-built with innovative features including iFly, North Star, bumper cars, and roller skating.
Pool heated? Alaska usable? Best RC option for Alaska pool use. 4 pools including one with a retractable roof (indoor/outdoor) — this is the main pool kids use in Alaska and it stays warm. Solarium (adults 16+, often opened to families in Alaska cold weather — ask at guest services). H2O Zone outdoor water park may be weather-dependent. 10 Jacuzzis. Multiple reviewers specifically highlight Quantum's covered pool as ideal for Alaska.
What Kids Care About
Can kids roam ship without adult supervision? Kids under 12 must be accompanied by adults or signed into Adventure Ocean. Ages 12/13+ (Teen Club) can come and go freely. In practice, Quantum's layout (large ship with many activity areas) means older kids can reasonably navigate independently once parents are comfortable. No explicit "no roaming" rule for older kids.
Kids club self-checkout? No for Aquanauts (3–5) and Explorers (6–8): parent must sign out.
Yes for Voyagers (9–12): parents can authorize self-sign-out.
Teen Club (12/13–17): come/go freely, no check-in.
Kids club activities (Adventure Ocean) Adventure Ocean leans toward free play with staff-facilitated activities: science experiments, arts and crafts, scavenger hunts, dodgeball, drama. Quantum-specific adds: kids can access some of the ship's signature attractions with the club. Hours: Sea days 9am–noon, 2–5pm, 7–10pm (after 10pm, hourly charge applies until ~1–2am). Port days: open continuously.
Self-serve ice cream hours Sprinkles soft-serve ice cream on pool deck, self-serve, until approximately 10pm. Two machines near the main pool area. Complimentary.
Self-serve pizza/sliders/pasta hours Windjammer Marketplace buffet: open 7am–11:30am (breakfast), 11:30am–3:30pm (lunch), 6:30–9pm (dinner). Includes pizza, burgers, pasta, stir fry. Sorrento's pizza available for grab-and-go slices during the day. Late-night dining options vary — check daily compass.
Self-serve breakfast without adults? Yes. Windjammer buffet is fully self-serve starting from 6:30am (continental) / 7am (full breakfast). Kids can go independently.
Pool for kids? Movies at pool? H2O Zone outdoor water park (interactive water play area with spray jets, geysers — good for younger kids); main indoor/outdoor pool with retractable roof. Solarium is 16+ but staff often open it to families in cold Alaska weather. Movies at pool: not confirmed — Quantum does not have a large outdoor movie screen equivalent to Princess's MUTS.
Other activities for kids Quantum-class signature features: North Star (glass observation capsule rising ~300ft above sea — stunning glacier views, book early), iFly (indoor skydiving simulation, additional fee), bumper cars, roller skating. Rock climbing wall, basketball court, pickleball, dodgeball. Arcade. Adventure Ocean activities. These unique features are a major reason families choose Quantum.
Movies available other times? In-room TV with movie options. Dedicated outdoor movie screen not confirmed on Quantum.
What Adults Care About
Daily / evening activities Quantum is the most activity-dense ship in this comparison. Daytime: iFly, North Star, bumper cars, rock climbing, fitness classes, casino, spa, cooking demos. Evening: shows, Music Hall with live bands, casino, bars, Two70° venue (large panoramic lounge that transforms for performances).
Entertainment (specific shows) No traditional Broadway show. Original productions in the Royal Theater: Starwater (multi-sensory live performance blending technology + emotion), Sonic Odyssey (immersive sound/light/choreography), Sequins & Feathers (Las Vegas to Paris glamour show). Music Hall: nightly rotating cover bands (classic rock, disco, big band). Two70° features robotic screen performances. Also: comedians, game shows (Love & Marriage), trivia.
Timed dinner seatings? Yes. Traditional: 5:00pm or 7:30pm (same table, same servers every night). My Time Dining: flexible arrival 5:30–9:30pm (walk-up or reserve via app). Windjammer buffet dinner: 6:30–9pm.
Vibe with small children Quantum is particularly well-suited for families with active kids who love tech and adventure. Multiple reviewers describe it as feeling like "an amusement park at sea." Kids with energy thrive here. That said, it's a large, active ship — if you have very young kids (under 5) who need naps and calm, the constant energy can be a lot. Families with kids 6–14 seem to have the best time.
