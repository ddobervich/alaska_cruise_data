Juneau, AK

Alaska's capital city · Whale watching, glaciers, hiking
Juneau sits at the base of forested mountains with the Mendenhall Glacier just 12 miles from downtown. It's one of the best ports in Alaska for whale watching — humpbacks feed in the waters of Stephens Passage through the summer. The Mt. Roberts Tramway and Mendenhall Glacier are top self-guided options.

Skagway, AK

Gold Rush history · White Pass · Yukon border country
Skagway was the main gateway for the Klondike Gold Rush of 1898. Today its preserved boardwalk downtown and the dramatic White Pass summit drive (2,900 ft above sea level) are the main draws. The Yukon suspension bridge and sled dog experiences are popular additions. Many options are bus tours into the mountains.

Ketchikan, AK

Salmon capital · Totem poles · Tongass rainforest
Ketchikan is Alaska's first port of call heading north, and often the last heading south. It's known for the highest concentration of totem poles in the world, the Lumberjack Show, and the Tongass National Forest — the largest national forest in the US. The town sits on a narrow shelf between the mountains and the sea.

Icy Strait Point, AK

Hoonah · Brown bears · Kayaking · Tlingit culture
Icy Strait Point is a private cruise destination owned by the Huna Tlingit people of Hoonah. It sits on Chichagof Island — one of the highest concentrations of brown bears on earth. Whale watching is popular here but most boat tours run $200+ per person; the options below offer great value with land-based and cultural experiences.

Victoria, BC

Canada's City of Gardens · Butchart Gardens · Whale watching
Victoria is the final port on many Alaska Inside Passage round trips departing from Seattle. The city is known for its English-influenced architecture, the world-famous Butchart Gardens (12 miles from the cruise terminal), vibrant Inner Harbour, and excellent whale watching. Ships typically dock at Ogden Point, a 20-minute walk from downtown. Note: prices are in USD.
